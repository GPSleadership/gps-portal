When Culture Looks Good on Paper but Nothing Changes
A practical TP3 roadmap for making values real in your organization
By Alex D. Tremble  ·  GPS Leadership Solutions
TP3™: Trust · Proactivity · Productivity → Profitability
Most organizations already have "the right words" on the wall. The problem is that day‑to‑day behavior doesn't match the values. At GPS we use TP3 to find and fix that gap:
TRUST
How we treat each other and share truth
PROACTIVITY
How we take ownership instead of waiting
PRODUCTIVITY
How we execute what we said we'd do
When trust and proactivity go up, productivity goes up — and with it profitability and business performance.
GPS FRAMEWORK:
ASSESS → DELIVER → SUSTAIN
Define success first. Then assess honestly, deliver targeted support, and lock in new behavior.
First: Define Success Before You Change Anything
If this shift worked, what would we see people doing differently in 6–12 months?
What does "full implementation" look like in observable behaviors and business results — retention, speed, customer outcomes, profit?
How would we know, in TP3 terms, that trust, proactivity, and productivity have genuinely improved?
Write this down. This is the target you'll assess against later.

ASSESS
Understand why words aren't becoming behavior

SUSTAIN
Make the new behavior the normal way you work
How do people interpret our values today? Have we defined what "respect" or "collaboration" look like in daily behavior — or is everyone guessing?
What feels easy vs. hard? Where are people already living the values? Where does it feel unrealistic, unclear, or risky?
What's blocking them? Time, tools, unclear authority, incentives, fear of speaking up, mixed signals from leaders?
Where is TP3 breaking down?
→
Trust: "I don't feel safe telling the truth early."
→
Proactivity: "Nothing moves unless a senior leader pushes it."

Productivity: "We add priorities but never stop anything."
Output: a short list of specific behavior gaps and practical barriers in your context.
Clarify the behaviors. Translate each value into "This looks like…" and "This does NOT look like…" — tied to TP3 and real business outcomes.
Targeted support, not generic programs.
Depending on your assessment:
–
Focused skill-building (e.g., candid feedback that still honors "kindness")
–
Coaching for key leaders who send strong signals
–
Practical tools: meeting templates, decision rules, feedback guides
–
Process tweaks so systems stop rewarding the old behavior
✓
Test for Deliver: A front-line employee can say, "I know exactly what they want me to do differently, and I have what I need to do it."
Nudges and reminders. Build prompts into existing routines: agendas, 1:1s, dashboards, emails, town halls.
Measure what matters. Behavior checks, short TP3 pulse items, business indicators tied to TP3 (project speed, customer scores, profit drivers).
Reinforce and hold accountable. Recognize concrete examples publicly. Address non-adoption — silence signals it isn't real.
Review at 6 and 12 weeks. What's working? What's not? What constraint needs fixing? Adjust and keep going.
