Fixing People Problems That Quietly Kill Profit

Step 2

DELIVER — Three Changes That Shift Who Owns the Problem

Redefine Ownership

Each leader on your team has one clear outcome they are accountable for — not a list of tasks, not a job description. One outcome. Make it explicit and name it in your next meeting.

Raise the Proactivity Standard

Define what proactivity looks like in your business. State it out loud, model it yourself, then measure it in every 1-on-1. What you tolerate, you teach.

Build the Feedback Loop

One 15-minute weekly check-in per direct report. One question: "What's at risk this week — and what are you doing about it?" No risk identified, no meeting needed.

Focus question: What is the ONE ownership gap that, if fixed in the next 30 days, would have the biggest impact on execution across your locations?

Trade-off question: What will you personally STOP doing so your leaders are forced to step into the space you've been filling?

Step 3

SUSTAIN — How You Lock the New Standard In

01
Review It Weekly

What gets measured, gets owned. Build a 5-minute ownership review into your standing leadership meeting — every week, without exception.

02
Name the Drift

When leaders slide back to waiting, call it — fast and without drama. Drift is normal. Silent tolerance is the real problem.

03
Reward Proactivity Publicly

What you celebrate, you replicate. When a leader catches a problem early, name it in front of the group. Make it the visible standard.
