HOW TO READ THIS DIAGNOSTIC (REALITY VS "TRUTH")
This diagnostic shows how people experience you, not who you are as a person. Raters don't see your intent, history, or stress level; they see your behavior in the moments that matter to them.
We treat this as stakeholders' reality, not "the truth." People act based on their perception. If Peter experiences you as reactive or hard to trust, that perception affects the calls he makes about structure, succession, and your future – regardless of what you meant.
The goal here is not to argue with every comment. The goal is to ask:
Whose perception matters most for where I'm trying to go?
How are those people currently experiencing me?
What would I need to do differently for them to experience me as the leader I intend to be?
Where your intent (E1–E2) and their impact (A/B/C/F/G scores and comments) don't match – especially with Peter and your potential successors – that gap becomes useful fuel for change, not a verdict on your character.
We will use this report to build a focused 90-day plan anchored in specific, observable behaviors, not personality labels.

90-DAY LEADERSHIP IMPACT PLAN (PRE-WIRED FOCUS)
Proposed Priority Behavior
In every significant task handoff or problem conversation, use a Delegation Brief: define the outcome, give guardrails, and deliver feedback privately and calmly – so your leaders own the work and Peter sees you leading, not just pushing.
Sample Metric
Metric: "Delegated responsibilities owned without rework or escalation"
Measurement:
Count of 3–5 key responsibilities you delegate in July–September (e.g., a category of customer escalations, a weekly sales meeting, a key report).
Track weekly whether each runs without you stepping back in or redoing the work.
Baseline: 0–1 responsibilities currently fully owned by others without your frequent intervention.
90-Day Goal: At least 3 major responsibilities fully owned by others, with 0–1 rework interventions per month from you.
Weekly Practices (examples)
Run the Delegation Audit once at the start of the 90 days; choose 3 activities to delegate now and 2 to develop for later.
For each delegated responsibility, complete a Delegation Brief and review it in a 1:1 conversation; send a short follow-up email capturing "definition of done," guardrails, and check-ins.
When an issue occurs, ask for the other person's view first, then use the SBI structure (Situation–Behavior–Impact–Next step) in a private, 3–5 minute conversation.
Start each week by blocking at least 2 hours for growth and succession work (e.g., coaching Esduard, planning outside sales efforts) and protect it like a customer meeting.
Once a month, ask Peter directly: "On a 1–10 scale, how much do you see me leading through others vs. doing it myself? What's one thing I can change next month?" Capture and act on his input.
Support & Accountability
You can run this plan on your own, but behavior change is easier with a neutral executive coaching partner who is not in the family or the business. GPS Leadership Solutions can support you with monthly coaching and an execution portal to track delegated responsibilities, weekly practices, and perception shifts from Peter and your team.
