The Opportunity

THREE GAPS WORTH GETTING AHEAD OF

Three years of flat revenue with rising costs is a slow leak. The cause is rarely one big thing. It is usually a few leadership habits that made sense when the company was smaller but do not scale.

Opportunity 01
Delegation and Follow-Through

When the person running the operation is also the best at most things, delegation can feel more like risk than leverage. Sergio likely knows the business well. What this diagnostic will clarify is whether he is delegating in a way that frees him up and develops the people around him, or whether he is holding more than he needs to. That distinction matters a great deal before adding headcount.

Opportunity 02
Coaching and Developing People

Adding staff is one decision. Getting results from that staff is a different challenge entirely. It requires a leader who sets clear expectations, catches problems early, and builds capability instead of just redirecting work. Before adding headcount, it is worth understanding exactly how Sergio develops the people he already has. That is the best predictor of what happens with the next hires.

Opportunity 03
Leadership Habits That Scale With Growth

The habits that got Wayside to this point are not automatically the habits that get it to the next level. A 5% annual revenue increase with new staff requires Sergio to lead differently than he has. That is not a criticism — it is simply how growth works. This diagnostic surfaces exactly where the gaps are before they become expensive people problems, so the 90-day plan he walks away with is specific, practical, and tied directly to your business goals.

The Diagnostic Framework

THE TP3™ FRAMEWORK: THREE LENSES

Each leader is assessed across three dimensions that determine whether the organization moves with them or stalls without them in the room.

T
Pillar One

TRUST

Do the people around this leader feel seen, heard, and valued? This goes beyond competence. It measures whether people feel their leader is present and invested in them as individuals, not just as role-fillers.

"Does this leader make you feel like you are part of the operation, or just an employee of it?"
P
Pillar Two

PROACTIVITY

Does this leader's presence and style pull initiative out of the people around them? High-performing teams take ownership because their leaders create the conditions for it, not because they are micromanaged into it.

"When this leader is not around, do things move or stall?"
P
Pillar Three

PRODUCTIVITY

Are the systems, expectations, and decisions this leader drives making it easier for the team to do great work, or harder? Operating efficiency at the leadership level is the multiplier behind everything else.

"Does working with this leader make your job easier or harder?"
Scope and Methodology

EVERY VOICE THAT MATTERS

One leader. Every perspective that matters, from ownership to the front line to the customers who depend on his follow-through. Quantitative ratings give you a scoreboard. The qualitative data gives you the story behind the scores.

Rater Design: 10 to 18 Total
  • Peter Batarse, Owner — your perspective defines the success criteria and anchors the entire assessment
  • Team members who work directly with Sergio — the people whose day-to-day experience of his leadership matters most
  • 3 to 5 key customers — where Sergio's leadership, responsiveness, and follow-through have the most direct business impact
What We Are Collecting
Quantitative Ratings

Specific behaviors rated on a 1 to 5 scale tied to a leadership profile defined at kickoff. Gives you a clear, trackable scoreboard over time.

Targeted Qualitative Questions

"Where does this leader show up best?" and "What one behavior would most increase your confidence in them?" Questions that surface the texture behind the numbers.

Confidential 1:1 Interviews

Conducted with Peter and 2 to 4 key stakeholders. These conversations capture nuance that surveys cannot reach on their own.
