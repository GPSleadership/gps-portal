Brave Conversation Blueprint

Lead hard conversations with calm, courage, and clarity — before, during, and after.
Use this when…
You've been delaying a conversation you know you need to have You feel angry, hurt, or anxious about how they might react Their behavior is hurting the team, customers, or results
Phase 1
Before: Slow Your Brain Down
Move from "reacting" to "leading" before you open your mouth
→ First, pause: Take 3 slow breaths.  If you're above a 7/10 on emotion, wait before starting this conversation.
1
Name What You're Feeling
Check all that apply — emotion is information, not fact
Angry
Disrespected
Afraid / Anxious
Embarrassed
Frustrated
Disappointed
Other:  
What story is my brain telling me?  "They're _____." / "This always _____." / "If I don't fix this, _____."
2
Accept Reality
Without judging it, describe what is actually happening
What did they actually do (or not do) that brings me to this conversation?
How are they likely to feel or react when I bring this up?
(e.g., defensive, ashamed, angry, shut down)
3
Clarify Roles and Intent
They have their role. You have yours.
Their role in this moment is:
My intent for this conversation is: "I want this conversation to help them / the team / the business by…"
My role as a leader is to:
Protect the team / customers / mission by:
Communicate the standard or boundary by:
Decide and follow through on next steps by:
4
Best-Case and Worst-Case Outcomes
Being honest about both shrinks fear

Phase 2
During: Consideration + Curiosity, Then Courage
Hold truth and respect at the same time
1
Consideration & Curiosity — Start by Understanding
Listen, reflect back, and make sure they feel heard before you respond
"I'd like to understand what was going on from your perspective."
"Walk me through what happened from your point of view."
"What were you hoping would happen?"
"What, if anything, made this hard to handle differently?"

2
Courage — State the Line Clearly

"Thank you for sharing that. And I need to be clear: [specific behavior] is not okay / doesn't work here."
"Here's the standard we have to hit going forward:"
"Here's why this matters for the team / customers / results:"

After: Protect Trust & Learn
Make sure the conversation built trust instead of eroding it
